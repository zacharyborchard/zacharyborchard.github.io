Gluten Free Pizza


English 314 Instructions

Cooking a gluten free cauliflower pizza provides a healthy, safe, and tasty alternative to eating a gluten free pizza from restaurants. Many restaurants such as Papa John’s and Domino’s offer gluten free pizzas but they are unsafe for people with celiac disease or sensitivity to gluten. Therefore it is often safer for those with celiac disease or sensitivity to gluten to cook their own pizza. We have developed a recipe that will provide those suffering from celiac and gluten sensitivity a safe, healthy, and tasty alternative. Our recipe breaks the recipe into five main steps: crust, sauce, toppings, assembly, and baking/serving.

Equipment

  • Oven
  • Cutting Knives
  • Cutting Board
  • Rubber Spatula
  • Mixing bowls
  • Oven Mitt
  • Baking Sheet
  • Parchment Paper
  • Cheese Grater
  • Measuring Spoons and Cups
  • Food Processor (Optional)

Ingredients

Crust
  • 2 heads of cauliflower (approximately 2 pounds)
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1/2 cup of cheese
  • Pinch of salt
  • ½ teaspoon oregano
  • 1 teaspoon italian seasoning
Sauce
  • 1 (15 ounce) can of tomato sauce
  • 1 (6 ounce) can of tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon ground oregano
  • 1 ½ teaspoons minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon italian seasoning
Toppings
  • Meat of choice
  • Vegetables of choice
  • 1 ½ cup of cheese

Other Information

  • Serving size: 4
  • Prep Time: 20 min
  • Cook Time: 1 hour
  • Total Time: 1 hour 20 min

Preparing the Crust


Preheat oven

Begin by preheating oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit.


Process Cauliflower

To simplify the process, frozen cauliflower rice can be used instead of a head of cauliflower. If fresh cauliflower is being used, begin by cutting the florets off of the head of cauliflower and place in a food processor. Chop until rice sized pieces are formed.


Steam Cauliflower

Place cauliflower in a microwave safe bowl and cover with a paper towel. Microwave for 4-5 minutes until softened. (If frozen is chosen, steam as instructed on package.)


Let Crust Sit with Salt

Place a clean towel or cheesecloth in a bowl and place the steamed cauliflower on cloth. Shake salt on top and let sit for 5 minutes.


Squeeze Excess Water

After 5 minutes, squeeze the cauliflower with the towel to remove all excess water. The more water that is removed the better the crust will turn out.


Add Eggs, Seasoning and Cheese

Remove cauliflower from the cloth and place in a mixing bowl. Add egg (beaten), mozzarella cheese, oregano, and italian seasoning. Mix until evenly combined.


Prepare Pan

Place a piece of parchment paper on the pan that is roughly the same size.


Spread on Pan

Use your hands to form a flat circle with the mixture onto the parchment paper. The final shape should be about a foot in diameter and a half an inch thick for best results.


Bake crust

Place pan with the crust on it into the oven. Set timer for 30 minutes and continue on to prepare the remaining ingredients.


Flip Crust

After the 30 minute timer goes off flip the crust over and bake for an additional 15 minutes. When the final timer goes off removes and let cool until the remaining steps are completed.

Preparing the Sauce


Combine Tomato Sauce and Tomato Paste

Pour the 15 ounce can of tomato sauce, 6 ounce can of tomato paste, and 1 ½ teaspoons of minced garlic into a medium sized bowl.


Combine Spices

Place 1 tablespoon of ground oregano, 1 teaspoon of garlic powder, 1 teaspoon of italian seasoning, and 1 teaspoon of paprika into a small bowl and slightly mix.


Mix Sauce with Spices

Pour the mixed spices into the medium bowl that the sauce is in. Stir sauce till spices are evenly mixed in.


Finished sauce

Preparing the Toppings


Dice Vegetables

Choose any vegetables you enjoy on your pizza, for this recipe onions and green pepper were used. Use the cutting board to dice chosen vegetables.


Slice Meat Toppings

Choose any meat you enjoy on your pizza, for this pepperonis were used. Use the cutting board to slice any meat.


Grate cheese if neccessary

Using a grater and a block of cheese, grate about 1 ½ cups onto the cutting board.


Chopped vegetables

Putting it all Together


Spread pizza sauce

Spread ¾ a cup of sauce onto the finished crust, you will have extra sauce but this can be kept in fridge for use later.


Spread cheese

Sprinkle the grated cheese evenly on top of the sauce.


Place toppings evenly around the top

Place the diced vegetables and any meat of choice evenly spread around the pizza.

Baking & Serving


Bake

Place completed pizza back into the oven for 10 minutes.


Broil

After 10 minutes, turn oven to low Broil and keep the pizza in for another 5 minutes.


Take Out of Oven

Remove the pizza from the oven. Be careful the pizza and pan will be hot. Be sure to use an oven mitt. Let cool for 2-3 minutes.


Cut and Serve

Enjoy!

Conclusion

Gluten free pizza provides a healthy, safe, and tasty alternative to regular pizza or gluten free pizza from chain restaurants for people with gluten intolerance or celiac disease. Now that you know how to create this delicious recipie, you can make it whenever you want and share it with friends!
